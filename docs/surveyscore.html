Quality engine

SurveyScore

Every member gets a public-facing score from 0 to 1000. It blends completion behavior, screen-outs, profile depth, tenure, and recent consistency — so partners get a clearer panel and members see why match quality matters.

How the number is built

Six factors are computed on a 0–1 scale, weighted, then scaled to 0–1000 (rounded). Weights sum to 1.0:

Factor Weight What it measures
Completion 25% Completes ÷ (completes + screen-outs)
Screen-out 20% 1 − (screen-outs ÷ attempts) — fewer screens help
Profile 15% Share of age range, gender, country, interests filled in
Quality 15% Currently aligned with completion rate
Tenure 10% Account age, ramping to full weight over ~30 days
Consistency 15% Completes in last 7 days ÷ completes in last 28 days

Tiers

Tier Score
New Below 400
Bronze 400–649
Silver 650–849
Gold 850–1000

Gold members can receive a small payout bump on credited completes (about +5% on the user share). Tiers do not promise more survey volume or a fixed income.

What screen-outs do

  • They do not add balance — only partner-verified completes credit.
  • They count in the attempt pool, so a high screen-out rate pulls completion and screen-out factors down.
  • Honest demographic answers and finishing when you qualify beat spam-clicking every link.

Repo name vs product name

The open-source backend lives in the SurveyScore repository. The member-facing brand is EarnCord. Same stack — different labels for operators vs users.
